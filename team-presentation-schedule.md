## Module Preference Survey

### Instructions for Students
Discuss the following during Week 2 Team Meeting:
1. Review all module topics (Module 2-12)
2. Decide on your team's top 3 preferred modules
3. Submit preferences (1st, 2nd, 3rd choice)

### Presentation Structure (25 minutes)

**1. Self-Introduction (~1 min)**
- Major, minor, years at CSUB, work experience, fun facts

**2. Presentation Objectives (~1 min)**
- Clearly state what the presentation will cover

**3. Related YouTube Video (~3 min)**
- Select a relevant video (movie clips, news, documentaries) to capture audience interest
- Do NOT play the video (copyright); show screenshot and explain why you selected it
- Video can relate to the case or the laws being discussed

**4. Module Summary (~18 min)**

- **Step 1: Case Selection & Introduction**
  - Select one case from the end of your assigned chapter in [*Employment Law: New Challenges in the Business Environment*](https://amzn.to/49xmOjo)
  - Explain why your team selected this case
  - Provide a brief overview of the situation (1-2 sentences)

- **Step 2: Law Summary**
  - Using both textbooks ([*California Employment Law 2025*](https://amzn.to/3Z8fQwo) and [*Employment Law: New Challenges in the Business Environment*](https://amzn.to/49xmOjo)), summarize laws related to your case
  - Explain differences between California Law and Federal Law
  - Only cover content directly linked to your selected case
  - **Bonus (+1 point)**: Share personal/family/friend experience related to the law

- **Step 3: Case Analysis (IRAC Framework)**
  - **I - Issue**: What is the legal question?
  - **R - Rule**: What laws or legal principles apply?
  - **A - Application**: How did the court apply the rule to the facts?
  - **C - Conclusion**: What was the court's decision?
  - Compare: What would happen under California law?

**5. Discussion Questions (~2 min)**
- Create 3 discussion questions related to the case
- Briefly explain their importance
- **These questions will be used for team meeting discussions during the week**
- Assign roles for role-based discussion (see [Online Team Presentation Rubric](online-team-presentation-rubric))

**Team Discussion Roles (4 members):**

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|
| Employee Advocate | Argues from the employee's perspective |
| Employer Representative | Argues from the employer's perspective |
| HR Manager | Focuses on policy implications, best practices, and facilitates discussion |
| Legal Advisor | Analyzes legal risks, compliance issues, and summarizes key points |

> **Example**: If assigned Module 2 (Worker Classification)
> 1. Select a case from [*Employment Law*](https://amzn.to/49xmOjo) Ch 1 (e.g., "Case 1.1 - Boston Scientific v. Mabey")
> 2. Summarize Worker Classification laws (only case-related parts)
>    - [*Employment Law*](https://amzn.to/49xmOjo) Ch 1: Economic Realities Test
>    - [*CA Employment Law 2025*](https://amzn.to/3Z8fQwo) Ch 5: ABC Test, Dynamex
> 3. Analyze the case using IRAC Framework
> 4. Create 3 discussion questions → Used for team meeting discussions
