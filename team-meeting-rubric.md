# Team Meeting Rubric

## Overview

Each team meeting is worth **15 points total**:
- Team Meeting Agenda: **10 points**
- Peer Evaluation: **5 points**

Throughout the semester, there are 13 team meetings, totaling **195 points** (16.55% of your final grade).

## Part 1: Team Meeting Agenda (10 points)

The Note Taker is responsible for submitting the team meeting agenda to Canvas by the end of each week.

### Grading Criteria

| Criteria | Points | Description |
|----------|--------|-------------|
| **Completeness** | 4 | All required sections are filled in with substantive content |
| **Attendance + Screenshot** | 2 | Meeting screenshot included; attendance accurately recorded |
| **Discussion Quality** | 2 | Discussion notes demonstrate thoughtful engagement with the topic |
| **Completion Codes** | 2 | All team members' peer evaluation completion codes are recorded |
| **Total** | **10** | |

### Detailed Breakdown

#### Completeness (4 points)

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 4 | All sections fully completed with detailed notes |
| 3 | Most sections completed; minor gaps in detail |
| 2 | Several sections incomplete or lacking detail |
| 1 | Major sections missing or severely incomplete |
| 0 | Agenda not submitted or nearly empty |

**Required sections vary by meeting type:**

**Team Meeting 1 & 2:**
- Basic information (date, time, team number)
- Attendance table
- Discussion notes for each agenda item
- Action items
- Completion codes

**Team Meeting 3-13 (Regular Meetings):**
- Basic information (date, time, module topic)
- Attendance with discussion roles
- Case review summary
- IRAC analysis notes
- Discussion question responses (all 3 questions)
- Key takeaways
- Completion codes

#### Attendance + Screenshot (2 points)

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 2 | Screenshot included with all requirements met; attendance accurately recorded |
| 1 | Screenshot included but missing some requirements (name unclear, no date/time) |
| 0 | **No screenshot = 0 points for entire agenda (suspected written-only meeting)** |

**Screenshot Requirements:**
- Zoom Gallery View showing all attending team members
- Each participant's Zoom name displayed as "First Name Last Name"
- Meeting date/time visible (Zoom header or device clock in screenshot)
- Screenshot pasted directly into the agenda document (above Attendance table)

> **Warning:** Submitting an agenda without a meeting screenshot suggests that the team did not conduct an actual online meeting. This may result in **zero points for the entire agenda** and potential academic integrity review.

**Note:** If a team member does not join the online meeting, they must be marked as **Absent**. Falsifying attendance is a violation of academic integrity.

#### Discussion Quality (2 points)

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 2 | Notes show thoughtful analysis from multiple perspectives; discussion roles clearly applied |
| 1 | Basic notes present but lack depth or role-based perspectives |
| 0 | Discussion notes missing or superficial |

**For Regular Meetings (3-13):**
- IRAC analysis shows understanding of the legal framework
- Each discussion question includes input from assigned roles (Employee Advocate, Employer Representative, HR Manager, Legal Advisor)
- CA vs. Federal law comparison is addressed

#### Completion Codes (2 points)

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 2 | All team members' completion codes recorded |
| 1 | Some completion codes missing |
| 0 | Completion codes section not filled |

## Part 2: Peer Evaluation (5 points)

All team members must complete the self and peer evaluation survey after each meeting. This survey assesses each team member's contribution and attitude during the meeting, including your own.

### How It Works

You will rate **yourself and all team members** on a 1-5 scale based on their contribution to the team meeting.

### Rating Scale

| Score | Label | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Little/weak effort | Detrimental to team |
| 2 | Insufficient effort | Met minimal standards of team |
| 3 | Sufficient effort | Contributed adequately to team |
| 4 | Very strong work | Contributed significantly to team |
| 5 | Excellent work | Was crucial component to team's success |

> **Note:** If a team member missed the meeting without any notice, mark them as "Little or weak effort" (1) for the record.

### Grading Criteria

| Criteria | Points | Description |
|----------|--------|-------------|
| **On-time Submission** | 3 | Survey submitted by the deadline with valid completion code |
| **Thoughtful Feedback** | 2 | Ratings are honest and fair; written feedback is constructive |
| **Total** | **5** | |

### Requirements

1. **Complete the Survey**: Access the peer evaluation survey link from the [syllabus](syllabus)
2. **Rate All Members**: Rate yourself and each team member honestly and fairly
3. **Provide Feedback**: Include constructive written feedback for each member
4. **Submit on Time**: Submissions after the due date will NOT count toward your grade
5. **Share Your Code**: Send your completion code to the Note Taker

### Important Notes

- **Survey link**: Available in the [syllabus](syllabus)
- The same survey link is used throughout the semester (bookmark it)
- If you need to modify your response, re-submit the survey and use the NEW completion code
- Late submissions are not accepted
- Your feedback will be considered when assigning grades for the team activity

## Tips for Success

### For Note Takers

1. **Download the template** before the meeting starts
2. **Take a screenshot** early in the meeting (Gallery View with all participants visible)
3. **Share your screen** during the meeting to take notes in real-time
4. **Use Zoom recording** and AI to help format notes after the meeting
5. **Paste the screenshot** into the agenda document before submitting
6. **Submit early** to avoid technical issues near the deadline
7. **Collect completion codes** from all members before ending the meeting

### For All Team Members

1. **Update your Zoom name** to "First Name Last Name" for accurate transcripts
2. **Come prepared** by watching the presenter's video before the meeting
3. **Participate actively** in discussions from your assigned role
4. **Complete peer evaluation** during the meeting before leaving
5. **Share your completion code** with the Note Taker immediately

### Using AI for Meeting Notes

After the meeting:
1. Download the Zoom transcript
2. Copy the transcript into ChatGPT Edu
3. Ask AI to format it according to the Team Meeting Agenda template
4. Review and edit for accuracy before submitting

**Sample Prompt:**
```
Using the attached Zoom meeting transcript, create a structured meeting note following this template:

[Paste the Team Meeting Agenda template]

Transcript:
[Paste the transcript]
```

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What if a team member doesn't show up?**
A: Mark them as "Absent" in the attendance section. Do not falsify attendance.

**Q: Can I submit late?**
A: Late submissions incur a 10% deduction per 24 hours. Plan ahead to avoid issues.

**Q: What if I forget to save my completion code?**
A: Re-submit the peer evaluation survey and use the new completion code.

**Q: Do I need to submit the Zoom recording?**
A: No, the recording is for your team's use only. Only submit the meeting agenda with the screenshot.

**Q: Why is the meeting screenshot required?**
A: The screenshot verifies that your team conducted an actual online meeting via Zoom. Agendas without screenshots will receive zero points.

**Q: When should I take the screenshot?**
A: Take it early in the meeting once all team members have joined. Use Zoom's Gallery View so everyone is visible.

**Q: What if our team has fewer than 4 members for discussion roles?**
A: Some members may need to take on multiple discussion roles.
